# My Wine Notes Privacy Policy

Effective July 21, 2026

My Wine Notes has no advertising, analytics, tracking, third-party SDKs, or
app-specific accounts. A user's wine library is stored on their device and,
when iCloud is available, in their private iCloud database.

## Information the app handles

The app handles the label photos, wine names, ratings, types, tasting tags,
occasions, notes, and dates that a user chooses to save. This information is
used only to create, display, search, edit, share, and synchronize tasting
cards.

My Wine Notes does not sell this information or use it for advertising,
analytics, tracking, or profiling.

## Camera, photos, and text recognition

Camera and user-selected photo-library images are processed on the device.
Apple Vision may suggest a wine name from text recognized on a label. The
recognized text is not sent to the developer or to a third-party AI service.

When a user saves a completed tasting card to Photos, that exported copy is
managed separately by the Photos app and the user's photo-library settings.

## Local storage and iCloud

The wine library is saved locally in the app's private container. If a user is
signed in to iCloud, the app uses Apple's CloudKit service to keep a private
synchronized copy under that user's Apple ID. The developer does not operate a
separate server and does not use the wine library for advertising or analytics.
Apple's handling of iCloud data is governed by Apple's terms and privacy policy.

## Sharing

The app shares a tasting card only when the user chooses a destination in
Apple's share sheet or saves a copy to Photos. The app does not sell user
information or share it with advertisers or data brokers.

## Retention and deletion

A wine remains on the device and in private iCloud sync until the user deletes
it. Deleting a wine in the app removes its local record and queues deletion
from CloudKit. Removing the app deletes its local data but does not by itself
delete synchronized iCloud data. Copies previously exported to Photos or other
apps must be deleted there separately.

Users can change Camera and Photos permission in iOS Settings and manage the
app's iCloud data through their Apple account settings.

## Changes and contact

This policy may be updated when the app's features or data practices change.
The current policy will remain available inside the app and at the public
Privacy Policy URL on its App Store page.
